Jens Motion Control

Jens Motion Controls alternative psych-rock sound (with a sprinkling of pop) endeavours to capture the vast ocean of emotions experienced when you leave adolescence behind and experience a coming of age. Their off-beat drums, psychedelic bass and haunting vocals are inspired by the funky sounds of Altin Gun, the moody wit of Father John Misty and the slow style of Alexandra Savior. Melbourne-based band members Joe Alexander and Ben Emms play and record all the music themselves, combining synth, bass, electric guitar and drums to create an eclectic sound which leaves no track sounding the same. Their lyrics aim to create a connection with listeners by exploring feelings people don’t normally want to talk about. Combined with spontaneous touches such as gongs, synth and unexpected vocal outbursts, their music has a freeness to it that embodies Alexander and Emms’ long-standing friendship and musical history. Jen’s Motion Control’s musical journey began in 2010 in the four-piece band Fighting Like Stags, where Alexander and Emms jammed together in a garage developing a pop-rock sound inspired by The Black Keys and Arctic Monkeys. The band went their separate ways in 2013, with Alexander and Emms moving from New Zealand to Melbourne and beginning to expand on their musical sound as a two-piece.
